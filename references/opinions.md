# Strong Opinions (Supported by Evidence)

## Study Abroad Creates Competitive Career Advantage
In an increasingly global world, cross-cultural communication is essential for professionals across nearly every industry. Students who study abroad practice this skill every single day. International experience makes candidates stand out on job applications and builds a global network that opens unexpected doors.

**Support:** AIFS has placed nearly 2 million participants since 1964. Career value is proven, not theoretical.

---

## Language Immersion is the Only Way to True Fluency
There's no substitute for living and learning within a community that speaks the language every day. Classroom instruction alone cannot accelerate progress the way total immersion does. The confidence gained from navigating daily life in another language (ordering food, asking directions) is something students carry forever.

**Support:** Ketina's story. Her conversational Spanish was recognized as fluent by native speakers after one semester, not years of textbook study.

---

## Study Abroad Creates Lasting Identity Shifts
Studying abroad is transformative in ways that normal college experiences aren't. Students develop greater adaptability, independence, and a clearer sense of self. This isn't a skill gained—it's a shift in who you are as a person.

**Support:** Dominique's exact words: "It made me have life truly began for me during that experience." Not just a good experience—a life pivot point.

---

## Friendships Made Abroad Are More Durable Than Campus Friendships
The shared experience of navigating a new place together fast-tracks deep connections. Study abroad alumni count people they met abroad among their closest friends, even years after returning home. This is uniquely powerful.

**Support:** Derek's story. Despite being an introvert entering a program where he knew no one, he made "friends for life."

---

## Study Abroad is Most Valuable for Curious, Adventure-Seeking Students
Students who thrive abroad are those open to new experiences. Whether exploring art in Florence, learning tango in Buenos Aires, or discovering marine biology in Sydney—study abroad rewards curiosity with discovery.

**Support:** Specific destination benefits produce measurable academic and personal growth.

---

## The Best Time to Study Abroad is College
College students have the most flexibility, the fewest obligations, and the most time to process and integrate what they learn. Post-college, competing obligations (career, family) make study abroad harder. College is the optimal window.

**Support:** AIFS data showing majority participants are college-aged.
