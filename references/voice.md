# Voice & Writing Style Guide

## Tone
- Personal, conversational, and warm
- Inspirational but grounded (not fake enthusiasm)
- Student-centric perspective
- Honest about challenges while celebrating wins
- Relatable and authentic

## Sentence Structure
- Start sentences with benefit or hook (not background)
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Mix sentence lengths for rhythm
- Ask rhetorical questions to engage readers

## Example rhythm:
"There's something about studying abroad that changes you. Not in a cliché way—in a real way. Students come back different. More confident. More curious. More themselves."

## What NOT to do
- Avoid "unlock," "leverage," "seamless," "world-class," "journey"
- No fake urgency or scarcity tactics
- No generic motivational language
- Avoid exclamation marks (except in student quotes)
- Don't oversell or make guarantees

## Formatting
- Use short headings that promise benefits
- Number lists when showing progression or priorities
- Use student quotes to prove points (they're more powerful than your claims)
- Bold key insights, not filler words
- Separate serious sections with lighter anecdotes

## Language patterns to adopt
- "Simply put:" transitions
- "The reality is:" for honesty
- "Think of it this way:" for clarity
- "Here's why:" for explanation
- Student voice in quotes: natural, first-person, specific

## What works
- Specific examples (not generic descriptions)
- Real numbers and timelines
- Student stories and outcomes
- Active voice, present tense when possible
- Direct address ("You'll," "You can")
