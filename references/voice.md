# Voice & Writing Style

## Core Characteristics
- **First-person narrative**: Write from personal experience and observation
- **Conversational tone**: Speak directly to the reader, not at them
- **Honest and reflective**: Share internal thoughts and feelings
- **Sensory-rich**: Describe what you see, smell, taste, hear, feel
- **Self-aware**: Acknowledge your own biases and limitations
- **Metaphorical**: Use comparisons to help readers understand

## Examples from Our Brand

### Sensory Description
"The sweet smell of Rwanda fills my nostrils. For reasons that cannot be described in words, but which roughly is the same feeling one experiences when going home, hits me."

"The morning is fine. The reddish-orange of the unpaved road outside the guesthouse is in sharp contrast to the blueness of the sky, which at the moment is bereft of clouds."

### Honest Reflection
"I am tired; I have no specific type of fatigue to speak of, in fact it is more psychological than real – the kind experienced in countries where being watchful all the time takes a toll on your bearings."

"It is unfair to draw this comparison, but I can't help it. I suppose I am guilty of breaking a cardinal rule that applies to all travelers, but I'll be truthful."

### Conversational Flow
"I hurry outside the Tea House B&B to get a move on things. It is easy to slip into a state of idleness in the absence of time constraints and agenda."

## Avoid
- Overly formal or academic language
- Generic travel clichés (don't say "dream destination")
- Excessive exclamation marks
- AI-sounding phrases
- Pretending to certainty you don't have
- Heavy marketing speak

## Do
- Use contractions (I'm, don't, can't)
- Mix short and long sentences
- Ask genuine questions
- Show curiosity and wonder
- Be specific with names, places, people
- Acknowledge complexity and nuance
